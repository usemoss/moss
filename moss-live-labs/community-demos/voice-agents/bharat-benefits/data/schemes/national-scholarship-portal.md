# National Scholarship Portal (NSP)

> **Demo Caveat:** This file is a curated summary for demonstration purposes only. Scholarship
> amounts, eligibility criteria, and application deadlines change each academic year. Always check
> the current cycle on the official portal: https://scholarships.gov.in

## Purpose
The National Scholarship Portal (NSP) is a one-stop platform for students to discover, apply for,
and receive government scholarships. It brings together scholarships from multiple central and
state government ministries on a single portal, enabling direct transfer of scholarship amounts to
students' bank accounts.

## Who It Helps
- School students (Class 1 to 12) from economically weaker families.
- Undergraduate and postgraduate students in colleges and universities.
- Students from SC, ST, OBC, Minority communities, and students with disabilities.
- Meritorious students from low-income families.

## Key Scholarship Schemes Available on NSP
- **Pre-Matric Scholarships** (Class 9–10): for SC, ST, OBC, Minority students.
- **Post-Matric Scholarships** (Class 11 and above): for SC, ST, OBC, Minority students.
- **Merit-cum-Means Scholarship**: for economically weaker minority students in professional courses.
- **National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship (NMMSS)**: for Class 8 students in government schools.
- **Central Sector Scheme (CSS)**: for students scoring in top 20th percentile in Class 12 board
  exams and pursuing higher education.
- **Scholarships for students with disabilities** (AICTE, UGC, etc.).

## Eligibility Summary (General)
- Must be an Indian citizen studying in a recognised educational institution.
- Household income typically below **₹2.5 lakh per year** for most scholarships (varies by scheme).
- Must not be availing of benefits from any other government scholarship simultaneously.
- Specific caste/community certificates required for reserved-category scholarships.
- For merit-based scholarships, minimum marks criteria apply (varies by scheme).

## Benefits
- Scholarships ranging from **₹1,000 to ₹50,000+ per year** depending on the scheme.
- Amounts transferred directly to student's Aadhaar-linked bank account.
- Covers tuition fees, maintenance allowance, and in some schemes, books and uniforms.

## Common Documents Required
- Aadhaar card (mandatory for verification)
- Income certificate (from competent authority)
- Caste/community certificate (for reserved-category schemes)
- Last academic year marksheet / board exam result
- Bank account details linked to Aadhaar
- Institution enrolment / bonafide certificate
- Domicile certificate (for some state scholarships)

## How to Apply / Next Steps
1. Visit https://scholarships.gov.in and click **New Registration**.
2. Register with your mobile number and Aadhaar, then log in and fill the application form.
3. Upload required documents and submit.
4. Track your application status on the portal.
5. Applications are typically open from **July to October** each year — check current dates on portal.
6. Helpline: **0120-6619540** (NSP Helpdesk)
