# PMKVY – Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (Skill India)

> **Demo Caveat:** This file is a curated summary for demonstration purposes only. PMKVY phases
> and sub-schemes (e.g., PMKVY 4.0) are periodically updated. Always verify current courses,
> training centres, and certification details at: https://www.pmkvyofficial.org and
> https://www.skillindia.gov.in

## Purpose
PMKVY is the flagship skill development scheme of the Ministry of Skill Development and
Entrepreneurship (MSDE), implemented by the National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC). It
aims to enable Indian youth to take up industry-relevant skill training and earn a government-
recognised certification to improve their employability or entrepreneurship prospects.

## Who It Helps
- Youth (generally 15–45 years) who want to learn a vocational skill and get certified.
- School/college dropouts seeking employment-ready skills.
- Workers already in a trade seeking formal recognition of their skills (RPL — Recognition of
  Prior Learning).
- Women, SC/ST, persons with disabilities, and minorities receive special focus.

## Eligibility Summary
- Indian citizen, typically between **15 and 45 years of age** (varies by sub-scheme).
- For **Short Term Training (STT)**: candidates with some education background; minimum standards
  vary by course.
- For **Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)**: workers already in a sector for 3+ years can get
  their existing skills certified — no formal education required.
- **Special batches** exist for migrants, hill/tribal/border areas, and north-eastern states.

## Benefits
- **Free skill training** in 300+ job roles across sectors like construction, beauty, healthcare,
  IT, retail, hospitality, agriculture, electronics, and more.
- Training duration ranges from a few weeks to 3 months depending on the course.
- Government-recognised **NSDC certificate** on successful completion.
- **Financial reward (monetary award)** for successful candidates in some tracks (amount varies by
  scheme phase — check current guidelines).
- **Post-placement support** and linkage to job fairs, apprenticeships, and industry placements.
- For RPL: free health insurance (Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana) for one year in some
  phases.

## Common Documents Required
- Aadhaar card
- Bank account details linked to Aadhaar
- Photograph
- Educational certificates (if applicable)
- Proof of prior work experience (for RPL track)

## How to Apply / Next Steps
1. Visit https://www.skillindia.gov.in or https://www.pmkvyofficial.org to find courses and
   approved training centres near you.
2. Search by location and sector/job role.
3. Register online or walk into any PMKVY-empanelled Training Partner's centre.
4. Complete training, appear for assessment conducted by a Sector Skill Council, and receive your
   NSDC certificate.
5. Placement assistance is provided through the training centre or NSDC's job portal.
6. Skill India Helpline: **1800-123-9626** (toll-free)
